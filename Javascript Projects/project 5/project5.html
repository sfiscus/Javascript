General Project Information

  • The instructor reserves the right to modify the specifications for any of these parts in writing, in an email, or verbally during class time. These changes to the spec will be required.
  • The project portion of Learning Unit 5 consists of five parts. Each part has it's own page and specifications, and each is worth 10 of the 50 total available points.
  • The course projects are not group projects. You need to do you own work completely.
  • If you need help on a project you should first ask your instructor. Each instructor will have their own procedure for getting help from them.
  • You may talk to classmates but do not share code in any electronic or physical form. Conversations with classmates about projects should be general and not contain details about solutions.
  • Don't sit down at a classmate's computer and type into their project.
  • Don't ask classmates for their projects.
  • Be very careful when you print project files while at Madison College (aka MATC). When you print go to the printer immediately and retrieve your printouts.
  • There is sample output shown for each part.
  • The instructor reserves the right to modify the project specifications in writing or verbally at any time. Non-attendance will not be an acceptable excuse for not completing the correct and final specifications. Changing specifications is a very real part of the IT experience.

Important Note

See the Course Syllabus regarding partial credit for Learning Unit #5 (LU5).  If you decide to not attempt one or two or three or four of the five Project parts, you must say so in writing at the time of submission in the comment in Blackboard.  Otherwise, the Learning Unit will be graded just as previous Learning Units have been graded, with zero points awarded if any of the five parts are producing incorrect output, until it is corrected and resubmitted.

NOTE:  A decision to attempt fewer than all five project parts of LU5 is irrevokable later. Do not submit LU5 with only one part completed, with the idea of later submitting further parts. The decision to attempt fewer than all five project parts cannot be changed later--it is irrevokable.
